Most Intermediate Appellate Races Offer No Voter Choice

Across 17 states holding non-retention elections this year for intermediate appellate courts, 93 of 146 races—or 64 percent—are uncontested. In the vast majority of those, a sitting judge will keep the seat for a term of six, eight or even ten years.
