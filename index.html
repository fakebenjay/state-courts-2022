Most Intermediate Appellate Races Offer No Voter Choice

Across 17 states holding non-retention elections this year for intermediate appellate courts, 93 of 146 races—or 64 percent—are uncontested. In the vast majority of those, a sitting judge will keep the seat for a term of six, eight or even ten years.

Forty Percent of Supreme Court Elections Are Uncontested

Eighteen states had Supreme Court seats coming open this year. Of 43 seats up for grabs, 18 races—41 percent—were uncontested. In six states—Idaho, Minnesota, Nevada, North Dakota, Oregon and Washington—all high court "races" this year have only one candidate.
